# Indang, Cavite: The Town of Many Springs

## 1. Historical Identity: "Walang Tinag"

Indang’s history is defined by its role as a stronghold during the Philippine Revolution. Its Katipunan name, **"Walang Tinag"** (Immovable), reflects the steadfast spirit of its people.

- **Bonifacio Shrine (Barangay Limbon):** The exact historical site where **Andres Bonifacio** was arrested in April 1897. While his execution took place in Maragondon, the pivotal moments of his capture and initial detention occurred here.
- **Establishment:** Indang was separated from Silang and became an independent municipality in **1655**. Its elevation and river systems made it a vital military and trade outpost.

## 2. Heritage & Architecture

The town’s identity is anchored by its centuries-old spiritual landmarks and dedicated spaces for local history.

### Historical Parishes & Chapels

- **Saint Gregory the Great Parish Church (Poblacion III):** Established in 1611, it is one of the oldest in Cavite. It is renowned for its rose-colored stone facade and its role as a sanctuary during the revolution.
- **Saint Vincent Ferrer Parish (Lumampong Halayhay):** A key spiritual landmark serving the community of the upland barangays.
- **Seven Archangels Chapel (Daine I):** A significant local chapel known for its peaceful atmosphere and community importance.

### Indang Community Museum

A dedicated space preserving the artifacts, documents, and stories of Indang’s revolutionary and agricultural past.

- **Location:** Poblacion, Indang, Cavite

## 3. Culture & Traditional Arts

Indang is home to a thriving weaving tradition that empowers local artisans and preserves indigenous crafts.

### The Art of Weaving

The town is known for its skilled weavers who use traditional looms to create intricate fabrics. This craft is preserved by:

- **MKC Weavers Association:** A local collective dedicated to maintaining the technique and providing livelihoods.
- **Yndan:** A contemporary brand (IG: @yndanph) that blends heritage weaving with modern design.

### Festivals

- **Irok Festival:** Celebrated every December 1st (Indang Day). It honors the **Sugar Palm (Irok)** tree. The festival features street dancing with costumes intricately crafted from irok fibers and leaves.

## 4. Produce & Agriculture

The fertile volcanic soil and cool climate of the upland make Indang a primary source of high-value agricultural goods.

| Product            | Local Significance                                                                                               |
| :----------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Kalamay Indang** | The town's signature sweet, sticky rice cake; famous for its smooth texture and rich coconut milk flavor.        |
| **Coffee**         | A top producer of **Robusta, Arabica, and Barako** beans in the province.                                        |
| **Dragon Fruit**   | Major plantations in **Brgy. Calumpang** and **Buna Lejos** have turned Indang into a regional dragon fruit hub. |
| **Kaong & Sasa**   | Indang is a major source of sugar palm fruit (_kaong_) and organic vinegar (_sasa_).                             |

## 5. Natural Beauty: Rivers and Springs

True to its nickname, **"The Town of Many Springs,"** Indang is characterized by its abundance of flowing water.

- **Natural Spring Resorts:** Famous for "flowing water" resorts (e.g., **Villa Colmenar** and **Rio Villa**) that utilize cold, chemical-free spring water diverted directly from the ground.
- **Pantihan (Balayungan) Falls:** Located in **Barangay Pantihan**, this is one of the last remaining "wild" waterfalls in the province, featuring a wide cascade over natural volcanic rock.
- **River Systems:** The town is bounded by the **Ik-ik, Banaba, and Labac Rivers**, which provided natural defenses throughout history.
